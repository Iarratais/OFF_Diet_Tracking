Is my information shared with third parties?

No. We store all the information on your device. No data is uploaded from the device without your permission.


Why is information shared with the Open Food Facts database?

If you choose, information about new food types is uploaded to the Open Food Facts database to allow for the improvement of the database. This will reduce the amount of times that an item cannot be found.


What permissions are used and why?

INTERNET: this is used to gather information from the Open Food Facts database and to upload new food items to the database.

CAMERA: this is used to get barcodes from the devices camera.

ACCESS NETWORK STATE: this is used to check if the device is online or not.


Why can't the app find foods that I scan?

Sometimes, the Open Food Facts database does not have the food that you scan. It is encouraged that you manually add these foods to recude the number of times this happens.
